The First Nike Product: A Revolutionary Beginning

Nike is one of the most recognized and influential sportswear brands in the world today. However, its journey began with a single product that set the foundation for its success.

Origins of Nike

Nike was founded as "Blue Ribbon Sports" (BRS) in 1964 by Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman. Initially, BRS acted as a distributor for Onitsuka Tiger (now ASICS), selling Japanese-made running shoes in the United States. However, tensions arose, and Knight and Bowerman decided to create their own line of athletic footwear.

The Birth of the Nike Cortez

In 1971, Blue Ribbon Sports rebranded as Nike, named after the Greek goddess of victory. Along with the new name came its first independent product—the Nike Cortez. Designed by Bill Bowerman, the Cortez was developed with the goal of providing superior comfort, durability, and performance for runners.

The Impact of the Nike Cortez

When the Nike Cortez officially launched in 1972, it quickly gained popularity among athletes and casual consumers alike. It was especially embraced by long-distance runners, offering better protection and durability compared to other running shoes available at the time.

The Legacy of Nike’s First Product

The success of the Nike Cortez laid the foundation for what would become one of the most powerful brands in the world. The shoe’s popularity has endured for decades, with Nike frequently re-releasing updated versions in various colorways and materials.
